### ORC Q1 summary

- The report of Q1 activities is based on the actions and priorities listed in the [2026 Program Plan](https://github.com/orcwg/orcwg/blob/main/governance/program-plan/2026-orcwg-program-plan-1.0.pdf) defined for the ORC Working Group. It is assessed the progress against the four pillars, and each of the objectives established for 2026 are marked in **green,** if they are progressing according to the expectations, **yellow**, there is some progress but not as much as we would like at this point of the year, **red**, for those in which there is no progress.  
- Awareness, education and thought leadership \- The group is progressing quite well in this area and there are two specific objectives in which we are not progressing as expected:  
  - Campaigns to promote the FAQ \- with the last guidance package release, the finalization of the first complete version has been slightly delayed, and the launching of the campaigns will happen in Q2. It was also highlighted that the FAQ work remains as a living asset to which the community is open to contribute and that we will sustain.  
  - Ambassador program \- we are supporting speakers who participate in conference when we are aware, but we haven’t formalise yet a program to support ambassadors. Some of the main challenges we are seeing is its implementation without adding unnecesary bureaucracy.  
- Technical development \- We are progressing well in attestations and due diligence work streams, and we remain active and committed to the open consultations in which the Woking Group can have a voice. Our effort on coordinating standardization activities is not at the same level as it was in the past.Clarifications done to the different points:  
- In the last Specification Committee it was agreed to level up the deliverables format and moving from white papers to Technical reports or Specifications. The Eclipse Foundation Specification Process support us in achieving this objective.  
- It was clarified the scope of the work that the Eclipse Foundation is implementing as part of the Tender won with the European Commission. The final report is due by end of June, but it includes recommendations, we have decoupled the development of the reports from the EC from the work in ORC. The connection is clear, but the reports are conclusions based on the work we are developing as a community.  
- We don’t have deadlines committed for delivering ORC work, but given the positive impact the white paper on stewards had for the guidance package, it could be wise to have something about due diligence released before the next CRA EG in June.  
- The lack of development in the standardisation space could make sense based on the current development status of those standards. It was also mentioned that the possibility of contributing to AI Act harmonised standards could make this objective move towards significant progress.  
- It was also mentioned that there are CRA standardisation events in which the European Commission is participating without any mention to open source, which represent a significant risk for the successful implementation of the CRA.  
- The contribution to open consultations process worked very well for the CRA guidance package, and we are trying to replicate it for other regulations like CSA2, but in case the community doesn’t proactively contribute the EF is analising the text to make some proposals.  
- Institutional Engagment \- The relationship with the European Commission in tht context of the CRA Expert Group is very active and works very well. However, establishing the relationship with member states is not easy, ADCO group was built in March and we are trying to strengthen the links with them. We currently have fluent conversations with Belgium, and Spain, and we have invited Cyprus to rely on us for any conversation about the role of open source, but they are still figuring out the internal structure to progress. We have also started communication with ENISA to support it with topics like SBOMs or the Single Reporting Platform, we are partnering with ECSO, ECMA and OWASP in this work. We haven’t progress in the Public Policy Engagement Forum, and we haven’t structure the registry of regulatory initiatives. Comments in the discussion:  
  - We should consider the CSIRTs to support them on how to interact with the open source community. We discuss the possibility of proposing the European Commission and entity in which open source is consulted and involved that meets regularly (yearly) to address the risk of dilute the relationship built based on specific cases.  
  - The role of ENISA was also discussed, we agreed that given their responsibilities under the CRA, and the attributions the CSA makes it is a good actor to cooperate with. It was also mentioned that the relationship built around ORC involves all members of the WG and not the Eclipse Foundation only in this particular context.

We didn’t finish the rest of the presentation, but it will be send by email and the members of the SC will decide whether we find a new slot to continue the conversation, postpone it until the next meeting or keep it offline by email.
