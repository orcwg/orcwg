### Approval of the minutes of the previous ORC Steering Committee call 

- Approved by the SC and will be uploaded to the governance part of the repository

### ORC Q1 summary

- It was presented the community representation progress:  
  - Q1 didn’t progress as expected in members recruitment, the biggest challenge was targeting members that can join as strategic, this implies longer decision making times, and therefore it resulted in no new members.  
  - The collaboration with associations is progressing well, it was requested by the Steering Committee members more communication to understand the extent of the conversations. It will be included as a topic to report periodically in the Steering Committee meetings.  
  - Finally the cooperation with other Open source groups is progressing, but still the cooperation with OpenSSF is not happening. We have found disagreement in some topics like attestations.   
- It was also presented the status of the key activities per role (Eclipse Foundation, WG members, and the open source community). In general there is progress in most of the areas identified, but some of them requires an extra push:  
  - Coordination on standardization activities \- this is not happening today in the WG and it is necessary to analyse how to make it more relevant as part of the WG activities involving all the actors.  
  - Recruitment also requires an important push, WG members need to help Eclipse Foundation team to recruit more members to achieve the objectives set. \- It was proposed to create a table to monitor membership and revenue per quarter, the Program manager will build it and share it with the group.  
  - Currently the FAQ is not that active, it was suggested during the meeting that the new AI SIG and the interplay with the CRA could be good topics to be added.  
  - The specification work started, and given the circumstances with the vertical standards not referencing the horizontal, specially relevant for vulnerability handling, there is an opportunity to make an impact here.

### Formalisation of working mode

- It was taken the opportunity of the creation of the AI Policy and Compliance SIG to formalise the working mode for the working group activities.  
- This proposal is not aiming to substitute any of the existing governance procedures already established in the [EF operations guide](https://www.eclipse.org/collaborations/working-groups/operations/), but to formalise the way the working group has been operating, that complements the previous the already established processes.    
- The proposal specifically in the definition of the deliverables, its acceptance and lifecycle. The proposal is available [here](https://github.com/orcwg/orcwg/blob/main/working-mode.md).  
- It highlighted the editor role and its relevance in the process of moving a deliverable from planned to work in progress.   
- It was discussed to add blog posting to the working mode, but since the process is a bit intense for SC, it was proposed to bring to the Steering Committee the planned communications, so the SC is aware and can help to refine the planning. The marketing plan presented at the beginning of the year included the proposal, but for the next meeting an updated plan will be presented.  
- After the discussion, the Steering Committee approved the proposal and was merged into the main branch of the GitHub organization.

### Interplay between Cyber Resilience and the AI Policy and Compliance SIGs

- It was proposed to maintain a single working group calendar adding a tag before the name of the event to identify to which SIG the event belongs \[Cyber SIG\] \[AI SIG\].  
- Since the audiences are different and to keep the channels as relevant as possible we are implementing two separated mailing lists:  
  - Cyber Resilience SIG will keep communicating through the existing list.  
  - AI Policy and Compliance will have its own mailing list.  
  - General WG topics will be shared in both mailing lists.  
- In terms of Github organization we will follow the principle of maximum relevance and work continuation. As an example CRA \- AI Act interplay will be hosted under the cyber resilience sig Github branch, but communication will happen on both mailing lists.
