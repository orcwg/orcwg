### CRA Expert Group status

- CRA expert group guidance package:   
  - Commission released an updated version on June 24th; most submitted comments were incorporated directly.   
  - Two outstanding issues remain:  
    - Replace "contacting the person maintaining the project" with "contacting the project using its established processes."   
    - Clarify reporting obligation differences between stewards and manufacturers — current draft still conflates them; same Section 9.1 text to be resubmitted.   
- It was also discussed the economic incentive for upstreaming: Maintaining local patches is consistently more expensive than contributing upstream; this self-correcting economic dynamic is worth communicating to manufacturers.  
- "Maintainer" vs. legal entity ambiguity: Concern raised that horizontal standards listing "open source maintainer" alongside legal entities is contradictory,  most maintainers are individuals, not organisations. Risk that large manufacturers overwhelm individual maintainers with compliance-driven communications (the "curl situation" cited as example).   
- Upstream reporting limitations: Some discussion in WG9 horizontals about adding limitations to the manufacturer's duty to upstream fixes; community position is to keep the requirement broad and not allow opt-outs. No confirmed proposals from the expert group itself.   
- Unmaintained projects: If a project has no active maintainer, the manufacturer placing the product on the market bears full responsibility, not a reason to weaken upstream reporting requirements.
